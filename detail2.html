LONG STORY SHORT

I initiated, designed, and built "Incourse For Learning" from scratch as an individual project

01 Research

I interviewed multiple young learners to understand their frustrations, learning challenges, and expectations.

02 Design

I designed the entire dashboard system from zero, building user flows, wireframes, and prototypes.

03 Development

I built the desktop and mobile versions using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, ensuring responsive behavior.

MARKET RESEARCH

To build a more effective learning product, I analyzed the gaps in the current e-learning market compared with user feedback.

Competitor
Coursera
Udemy
Skillshare
Strengths
Large course library
Strong brand
Good mobile app
Weaknesses
Confusing UI
Unclear progress
High cost

USER INTERVIEWS

I interviewed several students and professionals from various backgrounds.

Too Many Tools

I have to jump between multiple apps just to track my courses, notes, and deadlines.

Information Overload

Other platforms are too busy. Too many menus, I get overwhelmed before I even start.

No Sense of Progress

I'm not sure if I'm actually understanding the material, or just watching videos.

No Clear Path

I want to learn a new skill, but I'm confused about where to start or which course to take first.

OPPORTUNITIES

Based on the user pain points and market gaps, I mapped the insights to four main feature areas for the MVP.

Tailored Dashboard

A central hub that surfaces all courses, deadlines, and progress at a glance.

Progress Tracking

A clear visual system to track course completion and personal learning goals.

Note Organization

A simple, integrated way to take and organize notes related to specific courses.

Path Curation

A feature to help learners discover and build logical learning paths for new skills.

ACTIONABLE WIDGETS
